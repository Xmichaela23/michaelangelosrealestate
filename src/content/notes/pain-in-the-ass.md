Buying a home can feel like the biggest, scariest thing you've ever signed up for. The paperwork alone is enough to make you wonder if you should just keep renting forever. The inspections, the contingencies, the counteroffers — each one a small cliff you have to lean over and look down from.

And yet. There's something clarifying about it. Something that strips away the noise and asks you, plainly: what do you actually want? Not what looks good on Instagram. Not what your parents think you should want. What *you* want, in the city you love, at a price that doesn't keep you up at night.

The process will test you. It will ask you to be patient when you want to sprint, decisive when you'd rather hide, and honest when it's easier to pretend. That's the pain-in-the-ass part. It's also the freedom part — because on the other side of all that friction is a door with your name on it.

Finally, a place that's yours. Not borrowed. Not temporary. Yours.
